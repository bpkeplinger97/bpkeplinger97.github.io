League of Legends Champion Select Assistant

This project was done in collaboration with fellow Information Science Student Ryan at the University of Maryland and was made possible through the contributions of the various League of Legends subreddits. Our mission is to understand the thought process behind selecting a champion in League of Legends, we collected data from various League of Legends based subreddits along with several discord gaming communities. We will use the statistics we gain here to make informed decisions for new players to help ease the tensions of a new players' early time playing ranked. None of our work is currently monetized and this is purely a passion project, any inquiries may be made to bkeplinger97@gmail.com. Our languages of choice has been Rust and Python, Python providing robust data manipulation tools and Rust offering a fast, thread-safe language which is easy to use. (Though I am slightly biased, given I'm a rustacean.)

Response by region, unsurprisingly most responses came from North America and Europe, totaling to 4103 responses, or 87.3 percent. We did not post on Weibo to gather Chinese data due to language barriers, though we considered it.

Self-Provided Aggression levels, interestingly the average aggression was 3.66 out of 5 overall, indicating that players who responded to our survey were more aggressive than average during their play.

Mapping of Aggression to Rank without aggregation. The question of "What is your current rank?" was only asked to those who said their main play-mode is Ranked Draft, leaving us with 2275 responses. From that, we correlated the average aggression across each rank. This is with the small groups of Iron, Master, Grandmaster, and Challenger (which account for 57 responses total).

The average response of aggression to rank tier, with the small groups being aggregated. Iron was combined with Bronze while Master, Grandmaster, and Challenger were combined into a single category.

A mapping of Total Champion Picks to those champions' average aggression levels.
